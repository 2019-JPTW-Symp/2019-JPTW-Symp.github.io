WELCOME


 

2015年10月因緣際會下，日本聲學會(Acoustical Society of Japan) 之心理與生理聲學研究群首度將每月例行性研討會移到海外舉辦，地點為台灣新竹清華大學。承辦單位因而命名為「第一屆台日心理、生理、與電聲聲學交流研討會」。 2019年起，承蒙科技部(Ministry of Science and Technology, MOST)與日本科學技術機構(Japan Science and Technology Agency, JST) 支持，我們將連續三年繼續辦理此項交流研討會，並且聚焦於心理與生理聲學（Psychological and Physiological Acoustics），敬請各方前輩與潛力新秀鼓勵與支持！

本年度的主題是 "Inclusive Sound Design", 我們很榮幸邀請到日方9名傑出且有趣的研究者來訪，台灣方面也有由醫師與教授組成的堅實群組對應。希望本次交流能在從不同的角度探討聽力障礙、語音、音樂感知、與音訊處理，奠定台日兩地的合作關係。

The 1st Japan-Taiwan Symposium on Psychological and Physiological (P&P) Acoustics was held in National Tsing-Hua University, Taiwan in 2015. That was the first time for the P&P Acoustics Committee of the Acoustical Society of Japan to organize its monthly research meeting oversea. This year, we are resuming this conference and will organize it annually over the next three years (2019-2021). We welcome all people working in related fields to participate.

The theme of the conference in 2019 has focused on "Inclusive Sound Design", held by the teams from Japan and Taiwan. We've invited researchers and doctors from Japan and Taiwan, discussing the issue from different perspectives such as hearing impairment, speech, music perception and acoustic processing.
 

KEYNOTE SPEAKER


Rumi HIRAGA

Professor
Division of Industrial Technology
National University Corporation of Tsukuba University of Technology, Japan
Topic: Music for deaf and hard-of-hearing persons

Dr. Hiraga is a professor of Tsukuba University of Technology, where she teaches deaf and hard of hearing (DHH) students, following seven years of service at Bunkyo University. Prior to the academic job, she received Ph.D (Engineering) and started the research on music for DHH persons during her maternity and parental leave. Before that she was with IBM Tokyo Research including a year’s stay at Almaden Research Center. Her current interest is the improvement of hearing ability of DHH persons especially at music-listening by novel training using music games. She also leads a project of information support for DHH and visually handicapped persons at sport-watching. rhiraga.info
